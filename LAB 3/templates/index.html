The American Dream

As a First Generation American, I struggle to be both Mexican and American because I am split into the two cultures who seem to not accept either side of me. The only other people I can relate to are other Mexican-Americans. They know the struggle of having to achieve the American Dream. It is a huge stepping stone to be able to go to college. That is everything my parents ever wanted for me, but imagine having to tell your child you can’t afford to send them to school. I dedicated myself to my schoolwork; I did interviews, essays, and the whole college process without any help. In the end, my hard work paid off because I received an amazing scholarship to pay for college. Both of my cultures created my driven personality to succeed and work towards my goals. If I would end up with the same job my parents have, I am basically saying all of their hard work was for nothing. They immigrated here to give me a better life. I am my parents’ hopes and dreams.

Chicago Scholars

Chicago Scholars are academically ambitious, first generation college students from under-resourced communities in Chicago. Our seven-year program helps them to navigate the complex transitions into college, through college, and beyond to a career. College is tough. On average, just 48% of Chicago Public School students who enroll in college receive a four-year college degree within six years. By contrast, Chicago Scholars graduates 86% of our Scholars graduate from college in six years.
